8.5 Appendix D: Quality Assessment Criteria

8.5.1 D.1 Methodological Rigor (0-5 Scale)

5 - Excellent: - Clear research question and objectives - Robust study design - Appropriate sample size and power analysis - Valid and reliable measures - Comprehensive statistical analysis

4 - Good: - Clear research question - Appropriate study design - Adequate sample size - Valid measures - Appropriate statistical analysis

3 - Adequate: - Research question stated - Basic study design - Sample size mentioned - Measures described - Basic statistical analysis

2 - Poor: - Unclear research question - Weak study design - Small sample size - Questionable measures - Inadequate statistical analysis

1 - Very Poor: - No clear research question - No study design - No sample size information - No measures described - No statistical analysis

0 - Not Applicable: - Insufficient information to assess

8.5.2 D.2 Technical Innovation (0-5 Scale)

5 - Highly Innovative: - Novel methodology or approach - Significant technological advancement - Creative solution to complex problem - Potential for broad impact

4 - Moderately Innovative: - Some novel elements - Technological improvement - Creative approach - Potential for specific impact

3 - Somewhat Innovative: - Minor novel elements - Some technological advancement - Standard approach with improvements - Limited impact potential

2 - Minimally Innovative: - Few novel elements - Minor technological changes - Standard approach - Minimal impact potential

1 - Not Innovative: - No novel elements - No technological advancement - Standard approach - No impact potential

0 - Not Applicable: - Insufficient information to assess

8.5.3 D.3 Practical Impact (0-5 Scale)

5 - High Impact: - Direct practical applications - Widespread adoption potential - Measurable outcomes - Significant societal benefit

4 - Moderate Impact: - Clear practical applications - Some adoption potential - Measurable outcomes - Some societal benefit

3 - Limited Impact: - Some practical applications - Limited adoption potential - Some measurable outcomes - Limited societal benefit

2 - Minimal Impact: - Few practical applications - Minimal adoption potential - Few measurable outcomes - Minimal societal benefit

1 - No Impact: - No practical applications - No adoption potential - No measurable outcomes - No societal benefit

0 - Not Applicable: - Insufficient information to assess

8.10.3 I.3 Research Methodology Recommendations

8.10.3.1 1. Longitudinal Studies

  • Need: Long-term evaluation of visualization effectiveness
  • Approach: Multi-year studies with repeated measurements
  • Benefits: Understanding long-term impact and adoption

8.10.3.2 2. Comparative Studies

  • Need: Direct comparison of different approaches
  • Approach: Controlled experiments with multiple methods
  • Benefits: Evidence-based method selection

8.10.3.3 3. User-Centered Design

  • Need: Focus on user needs and preferences
  • Approach: Participatory design and user testing
  • Benefits: More effective and usable visualizations
